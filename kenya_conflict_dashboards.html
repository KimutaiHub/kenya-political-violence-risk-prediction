Conflict Pattern Explorer

A working view into twenty eight years of political violence records across Kenya, built to surface which event types cost the most lives and when the calendar itself becomes a risk factor.

Fatalities per year, 1997 to 2026
This view asks a single question: which kinds of events actually drive the death toll, and does that differ from which kinds of events simply happen most often. Frequent, low harm activity like protests looks very different under a microscope than rarer, high harm activity like armed clashes.
This view tracks violence across the calendar rather than across categories: which years spike, which months are consistently unsettled, and whether the general election cycle itself behaves like a predictable stressor on the system.
Source data, ACLED weekly conflict records for Kenya, 1997 to 2026, merged with KNBS population weighted density figures Prepared by Ctrl-Alt-Elite for the election violence risk project
